Intel

Headquartered in Santa Clara, California Intel Corp. is manufacturer and designer of semiconductor product. Intel manufacture hardware devices like CPUs, IPUs, GPUs, VPAs, FPGAs, AI accelerators and Memory and storage solutions. Company operates into four segments Client Computing Segment, Data Center and AI, Network and Edge and Foundry. Customer includes OEMs, ODMs, cloud service providers, industrial and communication equipment manufacturers and others. Intel has wafer manufacturing facilities in the United States (Oregon and Arizona), Ireland, and Israel, assembly and testing facilities in Costa Rica, China, Malaysia, and Vietnam, and packaging facilities in the United States (New Mexico), Costa Rica, Vietnam, and Malaysia.

Recent Transaction: Sold NAND business for $9.0B to SK Hynix citing high competition, low differentiation and cyclic pricing in the NANA flash memory business.

About Operating Segments

Company operates in four segment Client Computing Group (CCG), Data Center and Artificial Intelligence (DCAI), Intel Foundry. that manufactures wafers and related services for intel products and other services that includes Altera (51% stake sold to silver lake partners) and Mobileye businesses.

CCG includes platforms and processors that power personal computers, DCAI provides high-performance computing, AI acceleration, and infrastructure solutions, supporting data centres, cloud providers, and enterprises and Intel Foundry segment includes manufacturing of wafers and related services for intel products.

Q1'25 Result Summary

Published: May 4, 2025

Total revenue declined by (0.4%) to $12.6B primarily due to lower sales in CCG segment which was partially offset by increased sales in DCAI and higher revenue in Intel Foundry segment.

CCG segment revenue declined by (7.8%) to $7.6B, client revenue which consist of notebook and desktop was down (9.2%) to $6.5B (86% of CCG revenue) primarily due to lower client volume resulting from incremental customer incentives offered to certain customer.

DCAI revenue increased by 7.8% to $4.1B, primarily due to higher demand for edge processing unit and server hardware's (Xeon processor) due to rapid adoption of AI.

Intel Foundry segment revenue increase by 7.1% to $4.6B, driven by increased advanced packaging services and increased internal consumption of Intel seven wafers for Intel 3 and Intel 4 leading edge process nodes.

Gross margin for Q1'25 declined to 36.9% v/s 41% primarily due to higher period charges because of Gaudi AI accelerator inventory reserves taken in Q1'25 coupled with lower sell-through of previously reserved inventory and increased mix of Intel 7 and Intel 3 product which generally has higher server unit cost and lower margins.

Operating margin improved to (2.4%) v/s (8.4%), primarily driven by lower payroll related expenditures resulting from headcount reductions taken under the 2024 Restructuring Plan, which was partially offset by startup cost associated with the ramp up of Intel 18A (new generation chip).

Q2'25 Guidance:

Management has guided revenue to be in range of $11.2B to $12.4B v/s 12.8B reported in Q2'24 with a gross margin of 34.3% v/s 35.4% in Q2'24.

For FY'25, Company initiated the process of deleveraging its balance sheet which is evident as Intel trimmed down its capital investments from previously guided $20B to $18B and Operating expenses of approximately $17B, which is $500M lower than previously expected.
